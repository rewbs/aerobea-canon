# Moohfalanda

## Overview

Moohfalanda is a presidential republic founded in 1924. The nation has experienced a diverse political history spanning over 100 years, with leadership alternating primarily between the National Populist Party, Labour Party, Labour Alliance, and Liberal Conservative Party. The country's political system has seen both stable long-term administrations and periods of rapid leadership turnover.

## History

### Founding (1924)

Moohfalanda was established as a nation in 1924 with the election of Herbert Woupou Mooson as its first president on 1 January 1924. The nation was founded as a presidential republic from its inception, with democratic institutions established alongside the creation of the nation itself.

## Government and Politics

### System

Moohfalanda operates as a presidential republic where the President serves as both head of state and head of government. The presidency can see multiple terms, and presidents have left office through various means including retirement, electoral defeat, and death in office.

### Timeline of Presidents

#### 1. Herbert Woupou Mooson (National Populist Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1924 - 1 January 1934
- **Birth:** 26 March 1870
- **Death:** 7 July 1938
- **How ended:** Retired from office
- First President of Moohfalanda, serving for 10 years under the National Populist Party banner. Established many of the foundational structures of the presidential system. Lived four years after retirement, passing away at age 68.

#### 2. Kloto Mooson (Labour Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1934 - 1 January 1950
- **Birth:** 10 June 1879
- **Death:** 1 September 1976
- **How ended:** Retired after long tenure
- Succeeded Herbert Woupou Mooson with a 16-year presidency, the longest single continuous term in Moohfalanda's history. Led the Labour Party's first administration, implementing progressive policies. Lived to age 97, passing away more than a quarter-century after leaving office.

#### 3. Jetta Goodfleece (Labour Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1950 - 1 January 1957
- **Birth:** 2 October 1890
- **Death:** 28 December 1971
- **How ended:** Retired from public life
- Led a Labour government focused on expanding access to education during a 7-year administration. Known for progressive social reforms during the post-war period.

#### 4. Edward Jones (Labour Alliance)
- **Served:** 1 January 1957 - 1 January 1966; 1 January 1967 - 1 January 1969; 1 January 1973 - 1 January 1980; 1 January 1990 - 1 January 1995
- **Birth:** 6 January 1916
- **Death:** 22 February 2000 (note: data shows 1 January 2000, but died 22 February 2000)
- **How ended:** Multiple retirements and electoral defeats
- Unique figure in Moohfalanda's history with four separate presidential terms spanning nearly four decades. First term (1957-1966): 9 years of Labour Alliance governance. Second term (1967-1969): Brief 2-year return after Liosa Sadies was ousted. Third term (1973-1980): 7-year administration following Herbert Woupou Mooson III's death in office. Fourth term (1990-1995): Final 5-year term at age 74-79. Total time in office: 23 years across four separate periods. Died at age 84.

#### 5. Liosa Sadies (National Populist Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1966 - 1 January 1967
- **Birth:** 23 March 1914
- **Death:** 16 July 1997
- **How ended:** Ousted after protests
- Brief but controversial presidency that established a far-right government. Administration lasted only one year before being removed following popular protests. Died in obscurity three decades later.

#### 6. Herbert Woupou Mooson III (National Populist Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1969 - 7 July 1973
- **Birth:** 12 November 1920
- **Death:** 7 July 1973
- **How ended:** Died in office
- Grandson of the first president, served as a conservative reformer for the National Populist Party. Died suddenly of illness while serving at age 52, creating a succession crisis that led to an acting presidency.

#### 7. Greg Gherkin - Gherty There (Liberal Conservative Party)
- **Served:** 7 July 1973 - 31 December 1973
- **Birth:** 12 March 1928
- **Death:** 2 August 2023
- **How ended:** Acting term concluded
- Became acting president immediately following Herbert Woupou Mooson III's death in office. Served for less than six months during the transition period until the next scheduled election. Lived to age 95, passing away in 2023.

#### 8. James Conusi (Labour Alliance)
- **Served:** 1 January 1980 - 1 January 1981; 1 January 1983 - 1 January 1990
- **Birth:** 10 June 1935
- **Death:** 10 January 2024
- **How ended:** Lost re-election; later completed term and retired
- Two non-consecutive terms: brief first presidency (1980-1981) ended in electoral defeat, then returned for a longer second term (1983-1990) of 7 years. Total time in office: 8 years. Lived to age 88, passing away in 2024.

#### 9. Moo Moo Moo (Labour Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1981 - 1 January 1983
- **Birth:** 15 May 1930
- **Death:** 16 May 2004
- **How ended:** Replaced by Conusi after election
- Brief 2-year presidency between James Conusi's two terms. Succeeded Conusi after his first electoral defeat but was then replaced when Conusi was re-elected. Died the day after their 74th birthday.

#### 10. Marty Moolop (Labour Party)
- **Served:** 1 January 1995 - 1 January 2006
- **Birth:** 1 August 1965
- **Death:** Living
- **How ended:** Retired
- Led an 11-year Labour administration focused on digital infrastructure programs and technological modernization. Launched initiatives that transformed Moohfalanda's digital economy. Currently 60 years old (as of 2025).

#### 11. Joh Luis Jackson (National Populist Party)

- **How ended:** Completed long conservative administration
- Led a 16-year National Populist administration, the longest continuous presidency since Kloto Mooson (1934-1950). His long tenure marked a period of conservative governance in the 21st century. Currently 67 years old (as of 2025).

#### 12. David Harris (Labour Party) - *Current*

### Political Parties

#### National Populist Party
The National Populist Party has played a significant role throughout Moohfalanda's history, from founding president Herbert Woupou Mooson through the modern era. The party has ranged from mainstream conservative governance to more controversial far-right administrations (notably Liosa Sadies).

**Key figures:** Herbert Woupou Mooson, Liosa Sadies, Herbert Woupou Mooson III, Joh Luis Jackson

**Time in power:** 1924-1934, 1966-1967, 1969-1973, 2006-2022

#### Labour Party
The Labour Party represents progressive policies, workers' rights, and social reforms. Has held power for significant periods, often focusing on education, social welfare, and technological advancement.

**Key figures:** Kloto Mooson, Jetta Goodfleece, Moo Moo Moo, Marty Moolop, David Harris

**Time in power:** 1934-1950, 1950-1957, 1981-1983, 1995-2006, 2022-present

#### Labour Alliance
A center-left coalition or party closely aligned with Labour values but with distinct organizational identity. Most notably associated with Edward Jones's four presidencies.

**Key figures:** Edward Jones, James Conusi

**Time in power:** 1957-1966, 1967-1969, 1973-1980, 1980-1981, 1983-1990, 1990-1995

#### Liberal Conservative Party
A centrist or moderate conservative party. Only held power briefly during the transitional acting presidency following Herbert Woupou Mooson III's death.

**Key figures:** Greg Gherkin - Gherty There

**Time in power:** 7 July 1973 - 31 December 1973 (acting presidency)
